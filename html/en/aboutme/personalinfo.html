Personality

For iTalent I we had to do some personality tests. These are my results:

MBTI test Type: ENTJ Reflection: This is partually right, but not completely. I indeed like to take fast decisions, and handle things in a direct way, but I try to include other people in my decision so I also keep them in mind.
Eneagram personality test Type 3: Achiever Reflection: I have a score of 12 on type 3 and 11, 7 on type 2, so I'm both equally as much. It looks to me that I'm more a type 3 then a type 2. I like helping, but only certain people.
Witness By: Lydia DeRose Reflection: What she says is right. Mainly the first good point is correct. From the bad point I don't really think the first point is a bad one. I also do not a 100% agree with the last one.
Decision Strong points: Good in dealing with things and doing it efficient. I also like helping people. Points to work on: I need to try to not do everything a 100% perfect, and also agree with it more if it doesn't work out completely. Also appreciate other people more and appreciate the work they do more.
Competencies

After the personality tests we also had to do a couple of workshops and lectures.

Name workshop/lecture/guestcollege Reflection
Language training The language training consisted of a language test, in the form of a dictation. My language is not strong and not weak was the result. I was always a little bit above the average.
Anysurfer I thought this was a rather boring workshop, The explanation was too complicated for what the actual goal was. It could easily be explained in 30 minutes. Also a lot of the things that were said were normal and should be done when you make the website.
Talents day I did workshop 32, Fun @ Work. I thought the workshop was very well done, but the concept of it really bad. The workshop said that you should change job every couple of years, which is not at all realistic. Not a single employer would want to train someone who plans on leaving, and it wouldn't give you a very good CV.
MBTI MBTI gave me a couple feeling. I found it rather interesting, but at the same time not realistic at all. I also found the group very annoying (too loud). The only thing that really came out of it is that the test was rather accurate.
Language week I didn't catch a lot from this workshop. We sat decently in the back and the guest speaker didn't want to use the microphone. I could catch pieces and do the exercise, but I didn't get the big lines of what he was saying.
